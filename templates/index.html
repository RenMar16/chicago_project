Underlying Causes of Death
In Chicago Neighborhoods
Between 2006-2010

This project explored the underlying causes of death in Chicago neighborhoods between 2006-2010, with the goal of providing a means of identifying areas of need with regard to healthcare/healthcare coverage.



The dataset we used for our research contains the cumulative number of deaths, average number of deaths annually, average annual crude and adjusted death rates with corresponding 95% confidence intervals, and average annual years of potential life lost per 100,000 residents aged 75 and younger due to selected causes of death, by Chicago community area, for the years 2006 â€“ 2010. A ranking for each measure is also provided, with the highest value indicated with a ranking of 1.
